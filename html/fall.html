Fall Semester
Pre-Algebra & Algebra 1B

While moving through the semester, you should pay particular attention to your performance on assignments [Homework, Quizzes, Exams, etc.]. If you find yourself having a difficult time with the work, it is your responsibility to seek extra help.

Basic Skill #3: Seek Extra Help - whenever necessary

There is no excuse for not seeking help after school. ALL coaches at St. Benedict's support their student athletes in acheiving their academic pusuits. If you bring a note from your teacher (or tutor), your coaches will not penalize you or your teammates for arriving late to practice.

Establish a clear channel of communication between your teachers and coaches and you will be fine.

Mr. Marlow's FY Homework Help - Room 44

My Algebra students are encouraged to initially seek help from Mr. Marlow after school from 3:30 p.m. - 4:30 p.m. in Room 44 of the Old Building. Attendance records for these homework help sessions are recorded on a daily basis. This will enable me to verify that you are making the extra effort to be successful in your studies.

Students who demonstrate this effort by consistently attending Mr. Marlow's homework help sessions are far more likely to pass my courses than peers who do not. For any specific questions, please feel free to speak with or contact Mr. Jon Marlow (jmarlow@sbp.org).

National Honor Society

The National Honor Society (NHS) is an organization within the school specifically maintained for the purposes of providing extra help to students. The St. Benedict's chapter of the NHS is led by four student officers – a President, Vice President of Tutoring, Vice President of Services, and Secretary. Each NHS member is required to perform fourteen (14) hours of community service each semester. This service includes tutoring at St. Benedict’s which all of my students are encouraged to take advantage of. Questions about the St. Benedict’s Chapter of the National Honor Society may be directed to its faculty advisors, James F. Duffy (jduffy@sbp.org) or Michelle Tuorto (mtuorto@sbp.org). Winter Semester
Algebra 1A & Advanced Algebra

T S.Portal will serve as my medium for disseminating information to you for this course. Announcements, handouts, homework assignments, and sometimes quizzes will all be uploaded to this portal throughout the semester.

Important documents (homework assignments, handouts, quizzes, answers, etc.) can be downloaded to your personal devices or stored in your school Google Drive.

Basic Skill #4: Use the Course Website effectively.

This is a college preparatory school. Therefore it is our responsibility here at St. Benedict's Prep. to PREPARE YOU for academic life at the world's top colleges and universities. All college courses have some online component to them. Most have entirely web based learning management systems.

As such, your ability to effectively use this online resource will have a huge impact on your final grade. The onus of doing well in this course is on you, gentlemen. Navigate your way to the S.Portal each night. Complete your homework, utilize the online study aids that have been provided to you, and seek extra help when necessary.

Do I still need in class materials?

Of course you do! We will cover several topics from Holt’s Algebra Textbook. But you will not receive a text of your own for this course. ALL of the necessary handouts and lesson content are provided to you through the S.Portal. Therefore, YOUR NOTEBOOK and FOLDER are of unspeakable importance for my courses.

The better notes you take and the more organized you are, the better you will do on the homework assignments, which in the end, will directly impact how well you perform of your quizzes and exams. You are required to bring your notebook to class everyday. I will be checking for them.

Basic Skill #5: Carry the Appropriate Course Materials with you - every single day.

Required Course Materials

  • No. 2 Pencils.
  • Looseleaf paper [For some homework assignments].
  • Two (2) Marble Composition Notebooks [College Ruled - highly encouraged.
    • Spiral Notebooks - acceptable - high discouraged
    • Multi-Subject Spiral Notebooks - acceptable - highly discouraged
  • One (1) Folder - only for my course - properly labeled.

Optional [Suggested] Course Materials

  • One (1) Standard 12 inch Ruler.
  • One (1) Binder - to keep hold your folder and looseleaf paper -can be used for other classes.
  • One (1) Calculator - capable of handling scientific computation.

Compliance with the required course materials will be graded as one of your first homework assignmnts during the second week of each semester.

Contact me at your convenience
